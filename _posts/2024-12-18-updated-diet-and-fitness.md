## Exploring and settling on Keto

Starting a company is hard. Coincidentally, so is maintaining your weight while starting a company. During the summer I went up to around 187 lbs from 175 lbs, not too big of a deal but I'm not a fan of creeping weight gain, especially since I have been overweight for close to 30ish years. Seeing the weight gain creep back in August, and knowing I'd be working more, and since I enjoy mostly eating meat and staying low carb, I decided to give the [ketogenic diet](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK499830/) a try. Also, your brain on ketones is quite the [superpower](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8840718/). My only complaint with this approach is that it usually takes 2-3 months for your body to get used to running exclusively on ketones. You could fast or do a shit-ton of cardio to deplete your glycogen levels, but even then your body might not be used to running on ketones. I'm an impatient person, I wanted to run completely on ketosis in one month or less. Solution? Use Ozempic. Ozempic is a drug that's been gaining popularity recently for its weight loss benefits, it's a GLP-1 receptor that helps lower blood sugar levels and suppress appetite. You'll want to do your own research on GLP-1 agonists before giving it a try, I recommend following [Peter Attia's work](https://peterattiamd.com/ama64/). We don't know enough about the long term effects of using these drugs, but I was willing to take the risk for a month to kickstart keto. For that month I lost 10 lbs (from 187 to 177 lbs), but I was also very fatigued, I barely had any energy in my workouts or for work each day. What was also surprising and partly why I was interested in testing Ozempic, was the willpower benefits: my inclination to drink alcohol or do anything for quick dopamine hits was significantly reduced. Today I still have the occasional scotch, but only 1-2 drinks per week on average. After one month, and once I knew I was producing enough ketones, I stopped the Ozempic shots and started strictly following a ketogenic diet.

## Daily Routine

My daily macros on keto are as follows:

- **Fats**: Approximately 70-75% of total calories
- **Proteins**: Approximately 20-25% of total calories
- **Carbohydrates**: Approximately 5-10% of total calories

For a daily intake of 1880 calories:

- **Fats**: ~146 grams
- **Proteins**: ~96 grams
- **Carbohydrates**: ~23 grams

This was calculated knowing my Basal Metabolic Rate is around 1840 calories on average. I'm a big fan of supplementation as a way of getting the most optimal nutrition, but it's important to know your body and how it responds to different diets and supplements. ### Workout Routine

I increased the number of workouts per week from 3 to 6. I should add my body is slightly biased towards gaining muscle, even while losing fat. [Probably genetic](https://you.23andme.com/published/reports/82c6897d0c284e82a7a085904777d615/). I'm not sure if this is a good thing or not, but I'm not complaining. The workout routine is quite simple and follows principles from Jim Wendler's [5/3/1 approach](https://t-nation.com/t/5-3-1-how-to-build-pure-strength/281694). For 3 days out of the week I follow:

- 5x5 Deadlift/Squat
- 5x5 Bench Press
- 5x5 Military Press

A supplemental exercise for each core lift, and then an ab workout afterwards. On the other 3 days I'm either performing a HIIT workout on an assault bike, a hot yoga class, or some other cardio workout class. On the day I'm not working out I'll typically do some form of active rest like a long walk or hike. The key is consistency, which is why I'm not performing that many exercises per day. Sleep is also extermely important to recovery, I'm currently averaging 6-7 hours of sleep per night. If I'm having trouble sleeping I'll take a [melatonin supplement](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00CDABRUW/?coliid=I2EFJ0IJFBMO96&colid=OCRLL2WUBBTJ).

### Conclusion

After switching diets and maintaining consistent workouts, I'm currently hovering at 168lbs, which is not bad from 187lbs earlier this summer. I even have good [cum gutters](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K4wQM9o3nOY) now!

Most importantly, my energy levels are through the roof. I can plan high-level strategies, code, lift heavy shit, and still have energy for other things each day. The only thing I miss is jiu-jitsu, which sadly had to be cut to focus on my startup. I'm hoping I'll get back into it in the future, but for now this routine is working great. Hopefully this post inspires you to find a diet and fitness plan that works for you, even if your job is demanding.
